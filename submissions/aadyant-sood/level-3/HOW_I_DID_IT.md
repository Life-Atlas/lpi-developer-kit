## What I Did

I chose the Design & UX track and decided to build a personal health digital twin dashboard called LifeTwin. I started by exploring some dashboard layouts in Figma to understand spacing and structure, and then customized one template instead of building everything from scratch.

I removed all unrelated elements and redesigned the layout to focus only on health metrics like sleep, energy, and stress. Then I added a hero section to explain the idea clearly, followed by metric cards and a digital twin status section.

---

## Step by Step Process

1. Ran the LPI sandbox and explored the SMILE methodology output
2. Understood the idea of digital twins and how they represent real-world systems
3. Selected the personal health dashboard option
4. Picked a base UI template and removed unnecessary components
5. Redesigned the layout to include:

   * Hero section
   * Health metric cards
   * Digital twin sync status
   * AI-powered insight section
6. Iterated on text multiple times to make it feel like a real product
7. Added small details like status indicators and insights to make it more meaningful

---

## Problems I Faced

Initially, my design looked very generic and felt like just another dashboard. I struggled with writing the right text that didn’t sound repetitive or basic.

Another issue was understanding how to represent the “digital twin” concept visually. At first, it looked like just a health app, but I improved it by adding the sync status and insight section.

I also had confusion around structuring the GitHub submission and separating Level 2 and Level 3 work properly.

---

## How I Solved Them

I focused on simplifying the UI and removing unnecessary elements. Instead of adding more components, I improved the clarity of existing ones.

For the digital twin concept, I introduced the sync status and AI insight to show that the system is actively analyzing the user.

For Git issues, I created a separate branch for Level 3 and organized files in a clean folder structure.

---

## What I Learned

I learned that designing a digital twin is not just about showing data but about helping users understand patterns and take action.

I also realized that small details like wording, spacing, and hierarchy make a big difference in how a product feels.

Most importantly, I understood how design, data, and AI concepts can come together to create something meaningful.

---

## Final Thought

This project helped me think beyond UI design and focus more on how a system works and communicates with the user. Instead of just designing screens, I tried to design an experience that provides value.
